The marketing channels winning in 2026 are the ones everyone wrote off as dead.⁣
⁣
Start with direct mail. If a FedEx package shows up at your door, you're opening it. The physical world cuts through digital noise in a way no algorithm can touch.⁣
⁣
Email and SMS are the same story. HighLevel published data showing their emails hit around a 6% click rate. That's not a dead channel. People just gave up on it early.⁣
⁣
Live events might be the most underrated of all. Dinners, happy hours, conferences. Relationships built in person compound in a way nothing online does. There's a reason younger people are leaning back into college right now, and it isn't the lectures. They can feel that relationship-building is getting harder, not easier.⁣
⁣
SEO isn't dead either, it's evolving. Clicks are down, but brand impressions inside AI overviews are driving real revenue. People see your name, remember it, and convert later. If you're only measuring SEO by clicks, you're measuring the wrong thing.⁣
⁣
The boring channels keep working because human behavior hasn't changed as fast as the tools have.
